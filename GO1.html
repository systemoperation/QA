📖 KEY DEFINITIONS (from PDF)

🔹 Metals
Lustrous, malleable, ductile, good conductors of heat & electricity.
🔸 Non-metals
Dull, brittle, poor conductors (except graphite).
🔹 Malleability
Property of metals to be beaten into thin sheets (Gold is most malleable).
🔸 Ductility
Property of metals to be drawn into thin wires (Gold most ductile).
🔹 Sonorous
Metals produce ringing sound when struck → used in bells.
🔸 Amphoteric oxides
Metal oxides reacting with both acids & bases → salt+water (Al₂O₃, ZnO).
🔹 Ionic compound
Electron transfer from metal to non-metal (NaCl, MgCl₂).
🔸 Mineral
Naturally occurring metal-bearing substance in earth's crust.
🔹 Ore
Mineral from which metal can be extracted economically.
🔸 Gangue
Impurities like sand, clay associated with ores.
🔹 Alloy
Homogeneous mixture of metals or metal + non-metal (Brass: Cu+Zn).
🔸 Corrosion
Gradual destruction of metals due to air/moisture/chemicals. Rusting of iron.
🔹 Galvanisation
Coating iron with zinc to prevent rusting.
🔸 Roasting
Heating sulphide ore in excess air (2ZnS+3O₂→2ZnO+2SO₂).
🔹 Calcination
Heating carbonate ore in limited air (ZnCO₃→ZnO+CO₂).
🔸 Aqua regia
3:1 mixture of conc. HCl & HNO₃; dissolves gold & platinum (royal water).
